About the Project

What are these graphics?

These graphics are visual representations of data collected from clothing labels. The main inquiry for this project focused on displaying the vastness of the 'hyper-object' that is the globalised fashion industry.

What do the graphics show?

The graphics show the location data gathered from the clothing labels. The geo-visualisation shows the distribution of brand headquarters versus the distribution of 'Made In' Locations. The bar chart is an iteration of the data, highlighting the quantity per country of design versus manufacture data points.

How was the data collected?

The data was collected by inviting each member of my IxUx Masters class to contribute two clothing labels each to the project. I asked for the labels to be cut off their respective garments and placed in a collection jar in our shared computer lab in the University of Limerick.

What is next for this project?

This project has helped me to gain skills in data collection and interactive visualisation. I hope to develop a physical data visualisation that interacts with this digital platform. The skills I learned in this project will aid me in devloping my thesis project, looking at open-source data sets and responsive visualisations.
